Stress Hormone Management
CORTISOL CONTROL
High cortisol drives belly fat, raises blood sugar, worsens triglycerides & blocks fat loss. This plan addresses all of it.
⚠️ Why This Matters For Your Blood Results
HbA1c 56 mmol/mol — Chronic cortisol raises blood glucose directly, worsening your pre-diabetic HbA1c independent of diet.
Triglycerides 4.5 mmol/L — Cortisol triggers liver fat production (VLDL), which raises triglycerides. Lowering cortisol directly lowers TG.
ALT 65 u/L — Cortisol promotes visceral & liver fat accumulation. Reducing cortisol supports liver recovery.
Belly fat & waist — Cortisol specifically drives abdominal fat storage. This is the primary hormone to manage for your waist goal.

🏋️ WORKOUT CORTISOL RULES
⚡
HIIT Days (Mon & Thu)
High intensity temporarily spikes cortisol — manage with recovery
⏱
Cap HIIT sessions at 45–50 min max. Beyond 60 min, cortisol stays chronically elevated and begins breaking down muscle.
🍌
Eat within 45 min post-HIIT. A protein + small carb meal (e.g. Greek yoghurt + banana) blunts the cortisol spike fastest.
😴
Never train HIIT on poor sleep. Sleep deprivation + HIIT = cortisol surge that stores fat rather than burning it.
🌡
Add 5 min cool-down breathing after every HIIT session — box breathing (4-4-4-4) actively lowers cortisol within minutes.
🧘
Add Weekly Yoga / Mobility (30 min)
Most evidence-based exercise for cortisol reduction
📅
Schedule on Wed or Sun — your lower-intensity days. 30 min yoga shows consistent, clinically meaningful cortisol reduction.
🎯
Focus on: Yin yoga, Hatha, or restorative yoga. These activate the parasympathetic nervous system — the opposite of the cortisol stress response.
📱
Free apps: Yoga with Adriene (YouTube), Down Dog, or simply 30 min of slow stretching with deep breathing counts.
🚶
Daily 20-min Walk Outdoors
Nature exposure reduces cortisol 13–16% per session
🌳
Walk in green space whenever possible — parks, commons, countryside. Research shows nature exposure uniquely lowers cortisol versus treadmill.
📵
Phone away during the walk. Scrolling social media during walks maintains the cortisol stress response — defeats the purpose.
☀️
Morning walks are best — morning light resets your cortisol circadian rhythm, improving the natural peak-and-trough pattern.

😴 SLEEP PROTOCOL
🌙
7–9 Hours · Same time every night
Poor sleep is the #1 driver of elevated cortisol — and worsens both HbA1c and triglycerides
01
Fixed wake time — even weekends
Your cortisol circadian rhythm is anchored to your wake time. Sleeping in on weekends resets it badly. Same wake time every day is the single biggest sleep improvement.
02
Room temperature 16–18°C
Core body temperature must drop to initiate deep sleep. Cool room = faster sleep onset = more growth hormone = lower cortisol the next day.
03
No caffeine after 1pm
Caffeine has a 6-hour half-life. A 3pm coffee still has 50% active at 9pm, directly raising cortisol and blocking deep sleep stages.
04
Magnesium glycinate 200–400mg at bedtime
Magnesium directly lowers cortisol and promotes deep sleep. Safe, inexpensive and well-evidenced. Take with your last meal or at bedtime.
05
No alcohol — especially for your results
Alcohol fragments sleep architecture, spikes cortisol in the second half of sleep, and directly raises your triglycerides. Zero alcohol is the right call given TG 4.5.
💊 CORTISOL SUPPLEMENTS
🐟
Omega-3 (EPA+DHA) — 3–4g/day
Already in your plan. Omega-3 reduces both cortisol and triglycerides. Take with meals. High-dose fish oil linked with 25–50% TG reduction.
🌿
Ashwagandha (KSM-66) — 300–600mg/day
Best-evidenced adaptogen for cortisol reduction. Multiple RCTs show significant cortisol lowering within 8 weeks. Take in the evening with food.
⚠️ Discuss with your GP first — may interact with thyroid medications
🧲
Magnesium Glycinate — 200–400mg/night
Lowers cortisol, improves sleep quality, reduces insulin resistance. Particularly relevant given your HbA1c. Safe and inexpensive.
🌞
Vitamin D3 — 2,000–4,000 IU/day
Low Vitamin D is strongly linked to elevated cortisol and worsened HbA1c. Ask your GP to test your Vitamin D level — deficiency is very common in the UK.
⚠️ Always discuss supplements with your GP before starting, especially given your blood results. Some supplements interact with any medications you may be on.
